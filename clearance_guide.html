What is a Security Clearance?

A security clearance is a government background check that determines your eligibility to access classified information. Most government contracting jobs require some level of clearance.

Clearance Levels Explained

üü¢ Secret

Secret Clearance

Most Common Level

  • Required for most entry-level contracting jobs
  • Allows access to information that could cause "serious damage" to national security
  • Investigation takes 3-6 months
  • Valid for 10 years
Example Jobs: IT support, basic analysis, administrative roles
üü° Top Secret

Top Secret Clearance

Higher Level Access

  • Required for intelligence analysis and sensitive programs
  • Allows access to information that could cause "exceptionally grave damage"
  • Investigation takes 6-12 months
  • Valid for 5 years
Example Jobs: Intelligence analysis, cyber security, program management
üî¥ TS/SCI

TS/SCI Clearance

Highest Level

  • Top Secret + Sensitive Compartmented Information
  • Required for NSA, CIA, and most intelligence work
  • Investigation takes 12-18 months
  • Valid for 5 years
Example Jobs: SIGINT analysis, counterintelligence, special access programs

How to Get Your First Clearance

1

Find a Sponsor

You cannot get clearance on your own. A company must sponsor you. Look for jobs that say "clearance sponsorship available" or "must be clearable."

2

Complete SF-86 Form

This is a massive background questionnaire covering your entire life history. Be completely honest - any lies will disqualify you permanently.

3

Background Investigation

Investigators will contact your references, employers, neighbors, and family. They'll verify everything you put on your SF-86.

4

Adjudication

A government official reviews your investigation and decides whether to grant clearance. This can take months.

5

Clearance Granted

Once approved, your clearance is "owned" by your sponsoring company. You can work on classified projects!

Common Myths Debunked

‚ùå "I can't get clearance because I have debt"

Truth: Debt isn't automatically disqualifying. The key is showing you're managing it responsibly.

‚ùå "Only veterans can get clearance"

Truth: Anyone can get clearance if they pass the background investigation. Military service is helpful but not required.

‚ùå "I need a college degree"

Truth: Education level doesn't affect clearance eligibility. Skills and experience matter more.

‚ùå "Clearance is permanent"

Truth: Clearances expire and must be renewed. Secret lasts 10 years, TS/SCI lasts 5 years.
