What is a "Contingent Offer?"

This is the "golden ticket" that makes the contracting world spin.

It's a Real Job Offer... With a Catch.

A "contingent offer" is a formal job offer that is contingent (or dependent) on a future event happening. If the event happens, you have the job. If it fails, the offer is void.

The Most Common "Contingencies"

  • Contract Award (The Big One): This is the most common. A company (like Northrop Grumman) is trying to win the "MIDAS STUDIES" contract. They will give you an offer that says, "This job is contingent on us *winning* the MIDAS contract." This is how they build their team before they even have the work.
  • Customer Approval: "This offer is contingent on the *government customer* (e.g., the NGA) approving your resume and clearance for this specific role."
  • Clearance Crossover: "This offer is contingent on your security clearance being successfully 'crossed over' to our company's control."

This is What Causes the "Job Hop"

When a contract is "re-competed," your current company (Zeta) might *lose* the contract, and a new company (Northrop Grumman) *wins* it.

The new company (Northrop) now has to hire 50 people to do the *exact same jobs* that Zeta's employees were doing. The easiest way to do this is to give all 50 Zeta employees a contingent offer. This is why you see people "hop" from company to company while doing the same job, at the same desk, for the same customer.
