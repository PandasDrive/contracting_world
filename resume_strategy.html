NEVER submit your resume to multiple companies for the same contract. This creates confusion, makes you look unprofessional, and can actually hurt your chances.

üéØ Strategic Resume Submission

Government contracting requires a different approach than traditional job hunting. Here's how to do it right:

1. Research First

  • Identify the Prime Contractor for the contract
  • Find out which subcontractors are on the team
  • Check if the contract is currently being re-competed
  • Verify your security clearance level matches requirements

2. Choose Your Target

  • Prime Contractor: Direct relationship with government customer
  • Subcontractor: Often more specialized, smaller teams
  • Small Business: Set-aside contracts, more personal attention
  • Commercial Space: Growing sector, less traditional contracting

3. Timing Matters

  • Before Contract Award: Apply to companies bidding on the contract
  • During Proposal: Companies are building teams
  • After Award: Immediate hiring needs
  • During Re-compete: Both current and competing companies hiring

4. Clearance Compatibility

  • No Clearance: Apply to companies that sponsor clearances
  • Secret: Most entry-level contracting positions
  • Top Secret: Intelligence and sensitive programs
  • TS/SCI: Highest level, most opportunities
